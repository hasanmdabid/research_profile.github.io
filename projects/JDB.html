JDB – Half-Hourly Sales Forecasting

Deep learning-based time series forecasting for granular retail sales prediction across store locations

Duration: Oct 2024 – Feb 2025
Funder: Junge Die Bäckerei GmbH
Role: AI Project Manager / Data Scientist

Project Overview

This project developed a sales forecasting model to predict half-hourly sales turnover for various products and store locations. It aimed to improve upon the limitations of the current Random Forest (RF) model, which struggles with fine-grained predictions and sequential data. The project explores and evaluates several advanced deep learning models, including DNNs, LSTMs, GRUs, Mamba, and Hybrid models, which are better suited for handling complex time-series data.

The goal was to find a highly accurate model that could predict sales at a granular level, even with irregular sales patterns and frequent zero-turnover periods. The models were evaluated using a range of metrics (MAE, RMSE, WAPE) and different prediction approaches (direct, cumulative, and daily turnover) to ensure a comprehensive assessment. By demonstrating that deep learning models significantly outperform the existing RF method, this project provides a valuable solution for retailers to reduce costs, optimize inventory, and enhance customer satisfaction.

My Contributions

In this project, I played a key role in developing and implementing the sales forecasting model. I performed data preprocessing, feature engineering, and model selection to ensure optimal performance. I benchmarked state-of-the-art deep learning models, including DNNs, LSTMs, and GRUs, to improve prediction accuracy and evaluated them against the baseline Random Forest model.

I also explored transformer-based architectures such as Autoformer and iTransformer; however, their performance for short-term, high-frequency prediction was not competitive in this specific context due to the irregular nature of bakery sales data. The final solution delivered significant improvements over the existing RF-based system in terms of prediction granularity and accuracy.
