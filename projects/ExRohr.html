ExRohr

Duration: Sept 2024 – Jan 2026
Funder: ExRohr GmbH, Lübeck, Germany
Role: Data Scientist

Project Overview

The ExRohr project focuses on the development of an AI-powered system for the inspection and fault detection of sewerage pipes. Utilizing advanced machine learning algorithms and computer vision techniques, the project aims to automate the process of identifying defects and anomalies in sewer infrastructure. This innovative approach not only enhances the accuracy and efficiency of inspections but also reduces the need for manual labor, thereby minimizing costs and improving safety.

The system is designed to analyze video footage captured by inspection cameras, detecting issues such as cracks, blockages, and corrosion in real-time. By providing timely and precise information about the condition of sewerage pipes, ExRohr contributes to better maintenance planning and prolongs the lifespan of critical infrastructure in Schleswig-Holstein state, Germany.

My Contributions

I am working as a Data Scientist in this project. My main contributions include developing and implementing machine learning models for defect detection in sewerage pipes, optimizing algorithms for real-time video analysis, and collaborating with cross-functional teams to integrate AI solutions into the existing inspection workflow.

Additionally, I am involved in data preprocessing, model training, and performance evaluation to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the detection system. This includes benchmarking state-of-the-art computer vision architectures against domain-specific requirements for infrastructure inspection.
