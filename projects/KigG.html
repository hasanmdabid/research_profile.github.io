KIgG

AI-supported in-vitro diagnostics for the differentiated detection and treatment of autoimmune diseases

Duration: Sept 2024 – Jan 2026
Funder: Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF)
Role: Data Scientist / AI Project Manager

Project Overview

The KIgG project utilizes innovative testing methods that simulate immunological processes outside the body to identify characteristic patterns. This approach enables differentiated diagnostics that address the individual disease progression of patients. With KIgG, the project effectively bridges the interface between basic research and clinical application, thus making a significant contribution to the further development of precision medicine.

expandAI GmbH supports the project with its expertise in machine learning and AI-based digitalization. Prof. Dr.-Ing. Marcin Grzegorzek, founder and shareholder of expandAI GmbH, and Artur Piet, CEO of the company, contribute their technical and strategic experience to the development of the diagnostic technology. The BMBF's DATIpilot funding guideline, with its "Innovation Sprints" module, offers a flexible and rapid way to translate innovative ideas into practice. Simplified application processes and short project durations of a maximum of 18 months provide researchers with an experimental space to test forward-looking technologies. The KIgG project exemplifies how targeted knowledge transfer can generate concrete societal benefits from research results.

My Contributions

In this project, I am responsible for developing and implementing machine learning algorithms to analyze complex immunological data. My work focuses on creating predictive models that can identify disease patterns and support personalized treatment strategies for autoimmune diseases.

The role combines both technical AI development and project management, coordinating between the clinical partners at UKSH and the AI engineering team at expandAI GmbH to ensure that the diagnostic models meet both technical and clinical requirements.
