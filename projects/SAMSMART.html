SAM-SMART

Security assistance manager for the smart home – AI-based security analysis and protective measures

Duration: 2023 – 2024
Funder: Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF)
Role: Data Scientist

Project Overview

The goal of SAM-Smart is to improve the security of smart home devices and networks and to assist users in assessing and dealing with security risks. A key focus is on the development of AI-based security analysis and protective measures. The project follows a user-centered, iterative-incremental approach, aiming to develop, among other things, a voice-based assistance system. This system helps consumers assess specific risks of devices and make secure configurations.

Additionally, a privacy dashboard is developed to enhance data overview and control. At the core of the project is the "Security & Privacy Gateway," a technical component enabling the detection of IT risks and security incidents while resolving security-related disruptions. It actively alerts users to risks, provides information on security-related topics, and offers guidance. With the help of this gateway, several concepts are explored and tested, including the development of an "Ambient Security Toolkit" for risk assessment and the creation of a collaborative testbed for pentesting and black- & white-box analysis of smart home devices.

Another focal point of the project is researching and developing automated procedures for detecting security-relevant software flaws and suppressing unwanted information flows. This includes generating hotfixes when manufacturers do not provide updates. Moreover, privacy-preserving distributed machine learning methods for sensor correction are investigated — aiming to improve the quality of cost-effective sensors while ensuring data privacy. These procedures are tested both under laboratory conditions and in real-world environments.

My Contributions

My role in the project as a Data Scientist was focused on the development of AI-based security tools and the analysis of data collected from various smart home sensors and network sources. My research includes the development of machine learning models for detecting security vulnerabilities and anomalous behavior in smart home devices and networks.

I also contributed to creating a privacy dashboard that enhances user control over their data, and worked on privacy-preserving machine learning techniques to ensure that sensor data can be analyzed without compromising user privacy.
