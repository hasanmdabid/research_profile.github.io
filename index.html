About Me

I am a Data Scientist at the German Research Center for AI (DFKI) and a PhD candidate at the University of Lübeck, Germany, supervised by Prof. Dr.-Ing. habil. Marcin Grzegorzek. My doctoral research focuses on effective time series data augmentation algorithms for wearable sensors, funded by the German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD).

With a background spanning electrical engineering, mechatronics (M.Sc., University of Siegen), and over a decade of industry and academic experience across Bangladesh and Germany, I bring a unique interdisciplinary perspective to AI-driven healthcare and signal processing research. I have led and contributed to multiple BMBF-funded projects in explainable AI, food intolerance diagnostics, and AI security solutions.

My work has been published in top-tier journals including PLoS ONE, IEEE Transactions, Elsevier, Sensors, and Wireless Personal Communications, and presented at IEEE and ACM conferences worldwide. I am passionate about building AI systems that are not only state-of-the-art, but also interpretable, reliable, and deployable in real-world clinical settings.
